## Session 5 Playtest Analysis

### New Defense Capacity Formula Testing

**CRITICAL VALIDATION:** Defense Capacity = Proficiency **WORKS BEAUTIFULLY** ✅

**Evidence from Combat:**

**Round 1, Marble Titan attacking Lyra:**
- 2 HD attack
- Lyra (Defense Capacity 2) defended BOTH hits successfully
- **OLD formula (Capacity 1):** Would have defended 1, taken 1 HD + spell loss
- **NEW formula (Capacity 2):** Defended both, NO damage, NO spell loss
- **Result:** Felt HEROIC without being invincible

**Round 2, Granite Titan attacking Kael:**
- 3 HD attack
- Kael (Defense Capacity 2) defended 1 success, 1 fail, auto-failed last
- Lost armor completely but body untouched
- **OLD formula (Capacity 1):** Would have lost armor + body HD in same attack
- **NEW formula (Capacity 2):** Got to "fight back" twice before overwhelm
- **Result:** Felt like STRUGGLE, not instant annihilation

**Round 3, Multiple attacks on Torvan:**
- 3 HD attack, then 2 HD attack (different rounds)
- Torvan (Defense Capacity 2) defended partial amounts each time
- Armor depleted over MULTIPLE attacks, not single hit
- **OLD formula:** Armor gone in ONE attack
- **NEW formula:** Armor lasted 2-3 attacks, created attrition feel
- **Result:** Felt like ENDURANCE combat ("I'm holding the line!")

**Boss Titans (Defense Capacity 3):**
- Significantly harder to damage (Prof 3 = 3 rolls per attack)
- 4 HD attacks only dealt 1-2 HD after defenses
- Created boss "tanky" feel without HP bloat
- **Result:** Bosses feel TOUGH, not just big HP sacks

### Comparison: Old vs New Formula

| Attack Scenario | OLD (Capacity 1) | NEW (Capacity 2) | Difference |
|-----------------|------------------|------------------|------------|
| 2 HD attack | Defend 1, take 1 | Defend 2, take 0 | **Dramatic improvement!** |
| 3 HD attack | Defend 1, take 2 | Defend 2, take 1 | **50% reduction in damage taken** |
| 4 HD attack | Defend 1, take 3 | Defend 2, take 2 | **Notable survivability increase** |

**Dice Rolling Count:**
- Average combat: 3-4 attacks received per PC across 4 rounds = 12-16 total attacks
- Each attack: 2 defense rolls (NEW) vs 1 roll (OLD)
- Total dice rolled: 24-32 (NEW) vs 12-16 (OLD)
- **DOUBLED rolling, but still manageable** (took ~30 seconds per attack resolution, not slog)

### Did It Create "Blow for Blow" Feel?

**YES** ✅

**Examples:**

**Torvan vs Granite Titan:**
- *"The Titan's fist connects—Torvan's shield blocks the first strike [roll 1], deflects the second [roll 2], but the third shatters it completely [auto-fail]."*
- **Narrative:** Torvan fought BACK, actively defending multiple times
- **OLD narrative:** *"The Titan's fist CRUSHES through Torvan's shield instantly."*

**Lyra vs Marble Titan:**
- *"The Titan's memory-draining touch strikes—Lyra's magical armor FLARES [roll 1], holding. Strikes again—armor FLARES [roll 2], still holding!"*
- **Narrative:** Lyra's magic WORKED, visibly protective across multiple blows
- **OLD narrative:** *"The Titan touches Lyra. One hit defended, spell forgotten."*

**Kael vs Obsidian Titan:**
- *"Kael's armor absorbs the first shard strike [roll 1, success], then crumples under the second [roll 2, fail]."*
- **Narrative:** Armor gave Kael TWO chances, not one
- **OLD narrative:** *"The shard barrage hits. Armor gone."*

### Did It Create Dice Slog?

**NO** ✅

**Reasons:**

1. **Most attacks = 2-4 HD damage**
   - Capacity 2 = 2 dice rolled
   - Capacity 3 (bosses) = 3 dice rolled
   - **Average resolution time:** 20-30 seconds per attack (acceptable)

2. **Roll sequence is FAST:**
   - Roll d10 (or d8, d12, d6) → Check 5+ → Success/Fail → Next
   - No complex calculations
   - Clear binary outcomes

3. **Narrative pacing helps:**
   - Describing each roll as blow-deflected or armor-hit keeps engagement
   - Players FEEL each defense, not just "roll dice pool, calculate total"

4. **Didn't slow combat:**

- With Bulwark: Effectively Capacity 3 (same as bosses!)

**Recommendation:**
- Add: "Unused Compacts don't stack" (use it or lose it weekly)
