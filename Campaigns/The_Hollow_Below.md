# The Hollow Below
## An Affinity Story Arc for Levels 2-3

**Setting**: The Mortal Weave (Aeralon), the mining town of **Stonebridge** and **The Delve**  
**Recommended Party Size**: 3-5 players  
**Estimated Sessions**: 5 (2 hours each)  
**Playtesting Focus**: Dungeon crawling, puzzle-solving, environmental hazards, faction politics, mixed challenge types (combat/stealth/social/investigation), balanced party spotlight

## The Mining Town of Stonebridge

**Stonebridge** sits in the **Ironfoot Hills**, a region of rocky highlands rich with copper, iron, and occasional silver veins. Population: ~600 souls. The town exists for one reason: **The Delve**â€”an extensive mining operation that's brought prosperity for thirty years.

### The Town

**Geography**:
- Built on both sides of the **Cragrun River** (stone bridge connects them, town's namesake)
- North bank: Refineries, smelters, warehouses
- South bank: Residential district, market square, town hall
- **The Delve**: Large mining complex 1 mile west, employing 200+ workers

**Economy**: 
Mining. Stonebridge sends refined ore downriver to larger cities. Prosperity has grown steadilyâ€”until three weeks ago.

**Government**:
- **Town Council** (5 members: Mine Overseer, Merchant Guild Rep, Landowner, Militia Captain, Citizens' Representative)
- Leadership divided on current crisis (see below)

**Notable Locations**:
- **The Stonehammer Inn**: Two-story establishment, town's social hub, excellent meat pies

- **The Smelter District**: North bank, always smoky, metalworkers live here

## The Problem: What Lies Below

### Three Weeks Ago

Mine Shaft #7 broke through into **something else**.

The miners thought they'd hit a natural cave systemâ€”but the walls were too smooth. Too regular. The air smelled of dry stone and something older than memory. Against protocol, Foreman **Durg Blackiron** (dwarf, ambitious) led a small crew deeper to investigate.

They found:
- **Carved pillars** covered in geometric patterns (no recognizable language)
- **A massive sealed door** made of black basalt, 15 feet tall, covered in circular locks
- **Stone statues** flanking the doorâ€”warriors in archaic armor, faces blank

Durg, excited by the prospect of discovering treasure or rare minerals, ordered his crew to **break the seals**.

They succeeded.

**Big mistake.**

### What Happened Next

When the door opened:
1. The statues **moved**â€”they were guardians, not statues
2. A **low rumble** shook the entire mine
3. One miner was crushed when a guardian stepped forward; two more fled screaming
4. Durg barely escaped, dragging an injured man to safety

Since then:
- **Tremors** shake the town regularly (minor quakes, no major damage yetâ€”but growing stronger)
- **Cave-ins** have collapsed three mine shafts (12 workers trapped, 4 dead, 8 rescued)
- Strange **grinding sounds** echo from Shaft #7 at night
- Miners report seeing glowing runes deep below
- Two scouts sent to investigate haven't returned

**Current Situation**:
- The Delve is closed (economic disaster for the town)
- The Town Council is **divided** on what to do (see Factions below)
- Miners are terrified; families demand action
- No one knows what they've awakened or how to stop it

### The Truth (PCs Will Discover)

The chamber below is **The Hollow Below**â€”an ancient temple to **Khuldras, the Deep Foundation**, a near-forgotten lesser deity of earth, stability, and buried secrets.

**Khuldras** was worshipped 800+ years ago by a now-extinct mountain civilization. When they vanished, the temple was sealed by the last priests to prevent disturbance. The seals kept the temple's guardians dormant and its ley-node stable.

Breaking the seals:
- **Awakened the Sentinel Constructs** (stone guardians programmed to protect the temple)
- **Destabilized the Ley-Node** (causing quakes)
- **Began the Resonance Cycle** (if unchecked, will trigger a catastrophic collapse)

**The temple must either be properly re-sealed or its ley-node stabilized by someone with divine authority.**

Most importantly: **Multiple solutions exist**. The party can seal the temple, restore it, claim it, or negotiate. No "right" answerâ€”only choices with consequences.
