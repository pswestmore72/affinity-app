## Mechanical Stress Tests

### Test 1: Resource Depletion Spiral

**Goal:** Validate Rush/Steady/Resolve death spiral interaction when party is pushed to breaking point.

**Setup:**
- Multi-round combat against sustained pressure
- Track each character's HD, Resolve, and action choices
- Identify when players choose retreat vs continue vs desperation

**Expected Outcomes:**
- Rush used when HD low but Resolve available
- Steady used when Resolve low but HD healthy
- Death spiral creates "you should be running" moment at Resolve 1
- No death unless BOTH resources hit 0

### Test 2: Multi-Target Proficiency Scaling

**Goal:** Validate area spells and multi-select abilities scale appropriately with Proficiency.

**Setup:**
- Test Fireball-like area spell at Prof 1 vs Prof 5
- Track area size increase (+5 yards per 2 extra successes, max Prof √ó 5)
- Test multi-select ability (Chain Lightning style) target count scaling
- Compare damage output vs single-target focus

**Expected Outcomes:**
- Prof 1 caster: Small areas, few targets (low-level feels appropriately limited)
- Prof 5 caster: Larger areas, more targets BUT not exponentially broken (cap prevents runaway scaling)
- Single-target attacks remain competitive (bonus damage cap at Prof prevents multi-target dominance)

### Test 4: Priest & Sovereign Resolve Mastery

**Goal:** Validate that Priests and Sovereigns are demonstrably superior at Resolve management compared to baseline Steady action.

**Setup:**
- Party with Priest and Sovereign vs party without
- Track Resolve restoration: frequency, cost, effectiveness
- Compare baseline Steady (3 HD ‚Üí 1 Resolve, uses action) vs class abilities

**Expected Outcomes:**
- **Priests:** Power Word: Endure restores BOTH HD + Resolve at range (vastly superior support)
- **Priests:** Divine Conduit restores Resolve WHILE dealing damage (offensive restoration)
- **Sovereigns:** Internal Alchemy HD‚ÜíWill triggers automatically (no action cost)
- **Sovereigns:** Resource Cascade restores Resolve during successful attacks (combat-integrated)
- Both classes feel like Resolve specialists, not just "slightly better"

**Results:** [PENDING]

### Test 5: Social Combat Viability

**Goal:** Validate Social Duels as viable alternative to physical combat with distinct feel.

**Setup:**
- Negotiation, interrogation, or debate scenario
- Track: Does targeting Resolve feel different from targeting HD?
- Track: Are Influence/Lore/Awareness approaches balanced?
- Track: Do defensive options (Concede Point, Deflect, Walk Away) provide meaningful choices?

**Expected Outcomes:**
- Social combat feels mechanically distinct from physical combat
- Characters already wounded (low Resolve from combat) struggle in debates (resource interconnection works)
- Victory conditions (0 Resolve = concede, -1 Resolve = broken) create dramatic moments

**Results:** [PENDING]

---

### Test 6: Death & Dying Three States

**Goal:** Validate clarity of Collapse vs Dying vs Death mechanics.

**Setup:**
- Scenario forcing characters to each death state
- **State 1:** Character at 8 HD, 0 Resolve (Collapse - unconscious 0-2 days, NOT dying)
- **State 2:** Character at 0 HD, 3 Resolve (Dying - making Resolve checks each round)
- **State 3:** Character at 0 HD, 0 Resolve (Death - both resources failed)
- Track: Are states clearly understood? Any confusion?

**Expected Outcomes:**
- Collapse distinct from death (broken will, intact body)
- Dying creates tension (can rally on 6, death spiral on 4-)
- Death requires dual failure (reinforces "Resolve is critical stat" theme)
- Stabilize action (Basic 2+) provides meaningful ally intervention

**Results:** [PENDING]
